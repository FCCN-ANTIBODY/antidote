# Common cause — the found unit, the market surface, and the dedication

Serializes the operator's naming session so a cold context can pick it up; **shape, not settled
spec** (the register of civic-node #95/#97). This is the noun the storefront was missing. Couples:
`docs/faces.md` (the three faces — the shelf sells *this*), `docs/cascade.md` (extract → settle →
decant — the flush-down is the decant), `docs/roles.md` (the Atlas seam — who asks the judge),
`ARCHITECTURE.draft-0.md` (the lattice, the queue, the counsel gate), `bin/judge-constitution.mjs`

## The first unit that is found, not authored

Everything in this system up to now has been **authored and exact**. A constitution — a bottle — is
bytes *someone wrote*; change a comma and it is a different bottle; its identity is the hash of the
exact thing a person declared. That is the ratchet (guard #2): declared, never computed.

A **common cause** inverts the verb. Nobody writes it. It is the **meet** that two-or-more
constitutions are *found* to already share — the output of the comparison, not anyone's input. The
units feel different because the verb changed: constitutions are **declared**; common causes are
**discovered.** It is the first object in the system whose name is earned by *relationship* instead
of *authorship* — which is exactly why "constitution" would not name it and "common cause" does. A
cause is something you are found to **share**, not something you **hold**.

Its integrity is therefore not its bytes but its **provenance**. A constitution answers "what did
you write?"; a common cause has to answer "*whose* meet are you, and was it clean?" So its anatomy
is different from a bottle:

- **its parents** — the constitutions it is the meet of, by content hash;
- **the meet** — the shared terms themselves;
- **the counsel record, when it was not clean** (below).

It is still content-addressed like everything else, but hashed over **{parents, meet, counsel-if-
forced}** rather than over authored bytes.

## The meet — clean or forced

The **meet** is the shared floor of two constitutions (the lattice's meet — the greatest common
lower bound; `docs/faces.md`, and the lattice `permits` the judge already reads). It comes two ways:

- **clean / miscible** — *discovered*, honest, nobody touched it: two layers that settle into one
  were the same liquid all along. This is exactly what a lattice `permits` entry asserts —
  miscibility that was always true, found slowly, never manufactured.
- **forced / an emulsion** — the meet exists only because someone shook two unlike layers together.
  That shake is a **human act**, never the machine's: the **counsel gate**. A forced common cause
  carries that person's signature saying "I made this mix"; a clean one needs no such record.

Because the provenance travels with the cause — including the counsel act, when there was one —
**everything we have ever chosen to brew is brewable again.** A decision, once made and provenanced,
replays; nothing has to be re-litigated to be re-poured.

## Major common causes — how we scope the industries

The opportunity: some common causes are *so common* they become **how the service is marketed**.
Imagine a **political** antidote, an **emergency** antidote. These are so tightly bound to the
industries we sell to that they may as well be **subdomains** of the thing. Naming the major common
causes is how we scope the industries we address right now — a control we can **scale on our own
time.** There could be zillions; none is permanent by right; they persist while they keep selling.

This is the first time **specialization at the collection barrier is good**, because it subtracts
nothing downstream:

- a specialized server may **mount special UI** on a major common cause — we do not want one bland
  UI for everything;
- the **biggest servers still contain them all** (the complete indexes) — specialization surfaces a
  cause, it never removes it from the whole.

## Flush-down — flattening, and who holds everything

Intent (stated as a direction we want, near-mandatory in this ecosystem): **if you are not the
bottom rung, you are by nature archiving and flushing down.** We are flattening everything. The
bottom rung is the flattened, complete index; above it, everyone **decants downward**
(`docs/cascade.md` — decant is the down-hop pour, disturbing nothing it leaves behind). The biggest
antidotes therefore contain every cause; a specialized server is a surface on top of that whole,
never a fork away from it.

Current mechanism: the flush is a **pull request** (the one batchable GitHub-workflow job, never a
hosted worker). That is fine to leave as-is. Honest caveat, named not hidden: PRs do not foil
someone who clears their data only by pull-requesting **multiple times to different antidotes** —
the leak is real and acknowledged; the flattening is a direction, not yet an enforcement.

## Brew — the nonprofit's curation and moderation

What an antidote lets you **query** is deliberately gated. This is why it is set up as: *we will do
a contract with you, and you pay us something* — and, essentially, **we are moderating that.** The
501(c)(4) is the one who decides what it will **brew**: when a partner comes, we are making sure it
is *something we want to brew.* Moderation lives in this layer (and perhaps in the brew itself).

The by-product is the quiet asset: keep an antidote around, watch it keep selling, and you come to
**understand the demand and flow of information** in that sector — observability on the demand of
information *itself*, with nothing left out. **We can tell you what you are not hearing.**

## The dedication

We are dedicated to enabling the study of **consentful information.** Nothing contained inside was
given by any consent other than the one we hold, right here, **in the person's original words.** We
devote ourselves to finding the commonality.

This is **not** the social-media pitch. It sounds like "what people are saying elsewhere," but we do
not spy on what people *like*. We **pay them to say things**, with consent, and go viral — and what
we hold is not a like or a trace, it is **what they literally committed to**: feedback itself, in
their words, lots of it.
